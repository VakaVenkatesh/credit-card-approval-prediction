How a screening decision is made

Gate

Hard eligibility rules

Zero or negative income, implausible age, or employment history that doesn't add up gets declined outright — deterministically, without asking the model to guess at data it has never seen. The training set has no applicant with income below ₹26,100; a submission of ₹0 is not something the model has an informed opinion about.

Score

ML risk ranking

Applicants who clear the gate are scored by an XGBoost model trained on 36,457 real applicants and their repayment history, using a decision threshold tuned for this specific problem rather than a default 0.5 cutoff.

Explain

Reason codes

Every score comes with the top factors that pushed it up or down (via SHAP), so a flagged application is never just a bare percentage — an analyst can see why.

What this model can and can't do

Only 616 of 36,457 applicants in the training data are high-risk — that scarcity puts a real ceiling on how discriminative any model can be here, regardless of tuning. This system is built and evaluated around that constraint rather than around it: the decision threshold is chosen to concentrate manual review on a small slice of applicants while catching a meaningfully larger share of true high-risk cases than a coin flip would. It is a triage aid for prioritizing human review, not an automatic approval or rejection system.
